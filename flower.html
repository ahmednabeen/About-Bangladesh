Flowers of Bangladesh

The Flower of Bangladesh reflects the country’s natural beauty and rich biodiversity. The national flower of Bangladesh is the Shapla, or water lily (Nymphaea nouchali), a stunning aquatic blossom found floating gracefully in ponds, lakes, and wetlands across the country. Its soft pink or white petals spread widely over green lily pads, creating a serene and poetic landscape. The Shapla is not only admired for its beauty but also symbolizes purity and peace in Bangladeshi culture. It blooms mostly during the rainy season, bringing a calming presence to the countryside. Alongside the shapla, Bangladesh is home to many other special flowers that add color and fragrance to its natural environment. One of the most beloved flowers is the Krishnachura (Delonix regia), known for its fiery red-orange blooms that cover the trees during summer, turning roadsides into vibrant canvases of color. Its blooming season is eagerly awaited each year. The Sheuli flower, small and white with an orange stalk, blossoms mainly in autumn and is often associated with religious rituals and early morning fragrances. It releases a gentle, sweet smell that fills the air and brings a sense of freshness and nostalgia. Rajanigandha (tuberose), popular for its strong and captivating fragrance, is often used in weddings and ceremonies. Its long stems with clusters of white flowers bloom beautifully at night. The Beliphool (jasmine) is another popular flower in Bangladesh, known for its intense, sweet scent and delicate white petals. It is often used in making garlands and for personal adornment. Together, these flowers—through their beauty, fragrance, and presence in daily life—create a vivid portrait of Bangladesh’s floral heritage. Whether blooming in the wild, growing in home gardens, or used in festivals, the flowers of Bangladesh capture the soul of the land.

Shapla

Shapla, the national flower of Bangladesh, is a beautiful aquatic blossom known for its serene beauty and cultural significance. Also called the water lily, it grows in ponds, lakes, and rivers across the country. With its soft pink or white petals and floating green leaves, the Shapla symbolizes purity, peace, and resilience. It holds a special place in Bengali literature, art, and folklore, often representing the simple beauty of rural life. During monsoon, the sight of blooming Shapla is common in wetlands, adding to the natural charm of the landscape. It reflects the deep bond between nature and Bengali identity.

Krishnachura

Krishnachura, known as the Flame Tree or Royal Poinciana, is one of the most beloved and vibrant flowers in Bengal. With its fiery red and orange blossoms, it transforms the summer landscape into a burst of color. Blooming mainly from April to June, Krishnachura symbolizes passion, beauty, and the spirit of Bengali summer. Its large umbrella-shaped canopy provides shade and charm to roadsides, schoolyards, and parks. Though not native to Bengal, it has become deeply rooted in Bengali culture, poetry, and memory, often associated with childhood nostalgia and romanticism. Krishnachura is truly a visual celebration of nature in Bengal.

Sheuli

Sheuli, also known as Night-flowering Jasmine or *Parijat*, is a beloved Bengali flower that holds deep cultural and spiritual significance. Blooming in the early hours of autumn, especially during the time of Durga Puja, its small white petals with orange stems spread a sweet, delicate fragrance. Sheuli flowers fall gently to the ground at dawn, creating a carpet-like beauty. In Bengali tradition, they are often offered in prayers and used in festive decorations. Associated with purity and devotion, Sheuli evokes nostalgia and joy, symbolizing the arrival of Sharodiyo season and holding a cherished place in Bengal’s seasonal and spiritual life.

Rajanigandha

Rajanigandha, known as "রজনীগন্ধা" in Bengali, is a beloved flower in Bengal, cherished for its sweet, enchanting fragrance. Also called tuberose in English, this elegant white flower blooms at night, spreading a soothing aroma that symbolizes purity, love, and serenity. Rajanigandha is often used in weddings, religious ceremonies, and garlands, adding a touch of beauty and fragrance to special occasions. Its long, slender stems and waxy white petals make it visually graceful and spiritually calming. In Bengali culture, it holds a poetic charm, often mentioned in songs and literature as a symbol of deep emotion and timeless beauty.

Beliphool

Beliphool, also known as the Bengali jasmine, is a small, fragrant white flower cherished in Bengali culture for its beauty and sweet scent. It blooms in clusters, especially during the summer and monsoon seasons. Widely used in religious rituals, weddings, and traditional ceremonies, Beliphool symbolizes purity, love, and devotion. Women often wear it in their hair as a natural ornament, especially during festive occasions. Its calming fragrance is also used in perfumes and garlands. Deeply rooted in Bengali tradition and poetry, Beliphool holds a special place in the hearts of many, representing simplicity, grace, and cultural elegance.

Bastard Teak

Bastard Teak, known as Polash (পলাশ) in Bengali, is a vibrant and iconic flower of Bangladesh, celebrated for its fiery orange-red blossoms. Blooming in spring, especially around March, the Polash tree transforms the landscape with its brilliant colors, often called the “Flame of the Forest.” This deciduous tree holds cultural and poetic significance in Bengali literature and folklore, symbolizing love, passion, and the arrival of spring. Traditionally, its flowers were used to make natural dyes and Holi colors. Found in rural and forested areas, the Polash flower stands as a striking emblem of Bengal’s natural beauty and seasonal charm.

Sunflower

The sunflower, known as **“Surjomukhi”** in Bengali, is a bright and cheerful flower that symbolizes positivity, energy, and admiration. Its large golden petals and sun-like shape make it easily recognizable and loved across Bengal. Although not native to the region, sunflowers are now widely cultivated for their beauty and valuable seeds, which are used for oil and snacks. In Bengali culture, the sunflower represents warmth and hope, often featured in gardens and floral decorations. Its name, meaning “face of the sun,” reflects its natural tendency to turn toward sunlight, making it a poetic symbol of devotion and light.

Marigold

Marigold, known as **গাঁদা ফুল (Gada Phul)** in Bengali, is a vibrant and beloved flower in Bangladesh. With its bright orange and yellow petals, it symbolizes purity, beauty, and auspiciousness. Marigolds are widely used in religious ceremonies, weddings, and festivals like Durga Puja and Saraswati Puja, often crafted into garlands and decorations. Their strong fragrance and rich color make them a favorite in homes and temples. Easy to grow and hardy in nature, marigolds are also seen blooming in rural gardens and city parks alike, representing a cheerful and sacred part of Bengali cultural and spiritual life.

Silk cotton flower

The Silk Cotton flower, known as **"Shimul"** (শিমুল) in Bengali, is a beloved and iconic flower in Bangladesh. It blooms in vibrant shades of red and sometimes white, usually appearing in late spring and early summer. The Shimul tree is admired not only for its striking, large flowers but also for the silky cotton-like fibers inside its seed pods. This flower holds a special place in Bengali culture and literature, symbolizing beauty, love, and the arrival of the warmer seasons. Its bright red blossoms often decorate rural landscapes, making it a cherished part of Bangladesh’s natural and cultural heritage.

Foal foot

Foal Foot, known as **"Ghentu Phul"** (ঘেঁটু ফুল) in Bengali, is a beautiful wildflower commonly found in rural Bangladesh, especially during the late monsoon and autumn seasons. With its small, bright yellow blossoms resembling miniature sunflowers, it grows in open fields and fallow lands. Traditionally, it is associated with folk songs and rural festivities, symbolizing simplicity and natural beauty. Though not widely cultivated, Foal Foot holds cultural significance in Bengali literature and poetry. The flower thrives in moist soil and sunlight, adding a vibrant touch to the countryside. Its delicate charm reflects the rustic soul of rural Bengal.

China rose

The China Rose, known as **Jaba ful** (জবা ফুল) in Bengali, is a beloved and culturally significant flower in Bangladesh. With its vibrant red petals and lush green leaves, it is commonly found in gardens and homes across the country. The flower holds deep religious importance, especially in Hindu rituals, where it is offered to deities like Goddess Kali. Besides its spiritual value, the China Rose is admired for its beauty and simplicity. It blooms year-round in warm climates and symbolizes purity, devotion, and strength in Bengali culture, making it a cherished part of everyday life and tradition.

Champa

Champa, known as চাঁপা in Bengali, is a fragrant and beautiful flower deeply cherished in Bengal for its elegance and sweet scent. It comes in several varieties, including white (Shwet Champa) and yellow (Sonali Champa), each symbolizing purity and serenity. Often blooming in spring and summer, the Champa flower is commonly used in religious offerings, traditional rituals, and as a natural perfume. Its delicate petals and calming aroma make it a favorite in Bengali gardens and homes. In literature and poetry, Champa is often celebrated for its beauty, symbolizing love, devotion, and the essence of nature in Bengali culture.

Malati

Malati is a beautiful and fragrant Bengali flower, cherished for its delicate white petals and sweet scent. Often blooming at night, it is also known as *Night Jasmine* or *Madhu Malati* in Bengal. The flower grows on climbing vines and is commonly found in gardens and near homes, especially during the summer and monsoon seasons. Malati holds cultural and religious significance, often used in temple offerings and traditional ceremonies. Its calming fragrance and simple beauty make it a symbol of purity and devotion. In Bengali literature and songs, the Malati flower is celebrated as a metaphor for love and grace.

Gardenia

Gardenia, known as *Gandharaj* in Bengali, meaning “king of fragrance,” is a beloved flower in Bangladesh. With its pure white petals and strong, sweet scent, it symbolizes purity, love, and elegance. Blooming mostly in the summer, the Gardenia thrives in the warm, humid climate of Bengal. It is often found in home gardens and used in religious offerings, weddings, and cultural events. The flower holds a special place in Bengali poetry and songs, celebrated for its beauty and fragrance. Gardenia not only pleases the senses but also connects deeply with the emotions and traditions of Bengali life.

Kadam

Kadam, known as *Kadamful* in Bengali, is a fragrant and beautiful flower that holds a special place in Bengali culture and nature. It blooms during the monsoon season, filling the air with its sweet scent. The flower is round and ball-shaped, with tiny yellow-orange petals and a soft, spiky texture. It grows on the Kadam tree (*Neolamarckia cadamba*), which is native to South and Southeast Asia. In Bengali literature and songs, especially in Rabindranath Tagore’s works, the Kadam flower is often symbolized as a sign of love and longing. It is also associated with Lord Krishna in Hindu mythology.

Hasnahena

Hasnahena, also known as Night-blooming Jasmine or "raat ki rani" in Bengali, is a fragrant flower cherished for its sweet, intoxicating scent. Native to South Asia, this delicate white flower blooms at night, filling the air with a soothing aroma that lingers until morning. In Bengali culture, Hasnahena holds a special place in gardens and memories, often associated with romance, nostalgia, and quiet beauty. Though small in size, its fragrance is powerful and enchanting. It grows as a shrub, with clusters of tiny, star-like flowers. Hasnahena is not just a flower—it’s a symbol of serenity and timeless charm in Bengali life.

The flowers of Bangladesh beautifully reflect the country’s natural charm and cultural richness. The national flower, the shapla or water lily, symbolizes purity and peace, blooming gracefully in ponds and wetlands. Alongside it, vibrant flowers like the krishnachura with its fiery red blooms, and the delicate sheuli, known for its soft fragrance, add seasonal color to the landscape. Fragrant flowers like rajanigandha (tuberose) and beliphool (jasmine) are widely used in celebrations and religious events, carrying deep cultural meaning. These flowers not only enhance the beauty of nature but also hold emotional and spiritual value in everyday life. From wild fields to home gardens, Bangladesh’s flowers are a living expression of its heritage, seasons, and the gentle rhythm of rural life.
